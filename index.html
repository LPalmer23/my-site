Quantum Option Pricer — built for the Qiskit Fall Fest 2025 Hackathon (Quandela challenge).

LinkedIn Article Live Demo GitHub Slides

I built this project with Zack Coombs for the Qiskit Fall Fest 2025 Quandela challenge. We designed a discrete quantum walk that approximates geometric Brownian motion and used it to price European call options. The model uses an 8-qubit price register, 31 time steps, log-space bounds, and a CRR-style biased coin to match risk-neutral drift. I wrapped the engine in a FastAPI backend and Vite/React frontend and added a small AI assistant to explain how the model works. This project deepened my interest in quantum computing and how quantum algorithms can be used in finance.

Vertical Aeroponics Initiative — BU Climate Lab-funded research initiative

Project site

I became interested in aeroponics after learning how traditional agriculture contributes to pollution and food insecurity. With a $4.4K investment from BU’s Campus Climate Lab (CCL), my co-project lead and I installed two vertical aeroponic towers in BU's GSU building to research their yield, energy use, and feasibility in existing urban infrastructure. What excites me most is seeing the research turn into meaningful outcomes such as donating fresh produce to local shelters and showing students that sustainable solutions can be both hands-on and scalable.

Live demo

I started this project after learning that during peak ISO-NE electricity demand, the grid taps into its most carbon-intensive energy sources and charges the highest rates per kWh.
